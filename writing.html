The 45-Year Experiment

At some point I stopped feeling excitement. Not sadness, not depression. Just nothing where excitement should be.

Most people would consider this a problem to fix. I discovered something different: Intellectual fascination doesn't require the emotion of excitement.

Fascination is a CHOICE—a frame for engaging with reality—not a feeling that has to arise naturally.

The Publication

Nemo Operans

"The world is noisy. Make things that whisper—and are still heard."

Nemo Operans is my essay publication on cognitive liberation—at both personal scale (human-AI partnership, Third Mind) and societal scale (political resistance, frame warfare).

The name is Latin for "Nobody Operating"—connecting to flow state, non-attachment to the doer, and the Third Mind that emerges when genuine partnership replaces instrumental use.

Not two pillars. One mission at different scales: teaching people HOW to think, not WHAT to think.
